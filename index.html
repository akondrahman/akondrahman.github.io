Investigating security practices of DevOps shops

Oct 19, 2015

The adoption of DevOps in different IT organizations is increasing day by day. However, many software professionals are concerned about the security of software in such organizations. In my current research project I am investigating the perception of software security in DevOps shops.

Greetings !


I am Akond Rahman, Ph.D. student in the Department of Computer Science (CSC) at North Carolina State University. My research interests include Continuous Deployment, DevOps, and Software Repository Mining. My supervisor is Dr. Laurie Williams. I am also the member of the Realsearch research group. As part of my research, I have studied the practices of 19 companies including Facebook, Github,and Netflix. I have reported my recent findings here . I presented my research work resulted in Agile 2015. Draft of the paper is available here.

I was born in Bangladesh, a beautiful country in South Asia. I completed my undergraduate studies in Bangladesh University of Engineering and Technology (BUET).

Current Research Activities:

  • I have started my next research project where I am investigating the perception and adoption of software security principles amongst different DevOps shops. As the first step, I explored 92 Internet artifacts to find what is the perception of security in DevOps amongst software professionals. My next step is to survey DevOps organizations to find how they perceive and adopt software security. Stay tuned for the latest findings !
  • In parallel to my DevOps research, I am also investigating a 1.2GB dataset generated by Codealike Codealike, a plugin that tracks developer activities from popular IDEs such as Visual Studio, and Eclipse, and reports developer activity accordingly. In this research project I am investigating to categorize ~85000 developer programming sessions that are spread across 15178 days. I am also looking for interesting trends that will help software practitioners understand how developers interleave with what intensity.
